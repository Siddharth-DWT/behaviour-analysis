1. The "Think → Act → Observe → Write" Loop

  MiroFish's report agent doesn't just write a report all at once. It thinks about
  what it needs, goes and fetches that specific piece of information, reads it,
  then thinks again before writing the next part.

  For NEXUS: Your post-call report should work the same way. Instead of dumping
  all signals into one big summary, the system should think "what was the most
  important moment in this call?", go look at the data for that moment, 
  understand it, then write about it — one insight at a time. This makes the 
  report feel like a human analyst wrote it, not a data export.

3.  Memory That Grows Over Time

    MiroFish uses a knowledge graph that remembers not just facts but 
    relationships — and those relationships update as the simulation runs. 
    It remembers that Agent A mentioned Agent B in round 3, and that they fell 
    out by round 7.

    For NEXUS: Across multiple calls with the same prospect or client, NEXUS 
    should remember patterns. "This person always shows stress when pricing 
    comes up." "Rapport typically breaks down around the 20-minute mark with 
    this client." That kind of memory turns NEXUS from a single-call analyser 
    into a relationship intelligence tool.


4.  Running Two Things at the Same Time

    MiroFish runs two social media simulations in parallel — Twitter and Reddit 
    simultaneously — using two separate API keys so neither one slows the other 
    down.
    For NEXUS: Your heaviest tasks during a live call — generating narrative 
    insights every 30 seconds AND responding to urgent alerts instantly — 
    should run separately, not in a queue. If a buying signal fires at the 
    exact moment the 30-second narrative is being generated, the alert 
    shouldn't have to wait. Two separate lanes, two separate processes.

5.  Writing Down Everything That Happened and Why

    MiroFish keeps a detailed log of every single action every agent took — 
    what they posted, when, why, in response to what. This means you can 
    always trace back any finding in the report to the exact moment that 
    caused it.
    For NEXUS: Every alert, every insight, every signal that fires should be 
    logged with the reason it fired. Not just "stress detected at 14:32" but 
    "stress detected at 14:32 because pitch went up 18%, jitter went up, and 
    fillers spiked — all within 3 seconds of the pricing question." If a 
    client ever challenges a finding, you can show your work.
